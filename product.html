01 · The loop

The whole product is four screens.

Capture takes the task. Classification reads it and shows the evidence. History holds the pattern. Empty state is what a first time user sees. That is the surface area. There is nothing else to learn.

One input. One primary action. The screen is built for one handed thumb use on a phone, so the field and the submit button sit in the lower part of the viewport. Nothing else competes for attention here.

02 · The classifier

What Centaur reads, and what it shows you.

The classifier looks at the verbs you used, where they sat in the sentence, and whether you reserved a checkpoint for yourself. The phrase that produced the reading is named on the screen. If the reading is wrong, you can mark it.

03 · The three controls

Built to stay honest with the person using it.

Three controls ship in the first build. They are not polish. They exist so the product cannot quietly become the thing it set out to fix.

01 · TRANSPARENT

Every verdict shows its evidence.

There is no black box reading. The reasoning that produced the classification is always on screen, in plain language. The phrase Centaur matched on is named.

A verdict screen without evidence does not exist.

02 · OVERRIDABLE

You can override any reading.

Mark any classification as wrong, on every verdict. The override is logged, so your record stays honest about both what Centaur read and what you said it was.

Verbatim label: Mark this classification as wrong.

03 · OPTIONAL

The drift indicator is optional.

Turn it off whenever you want. Centaur keeps classifying and keeps logging. It just stops surfacing the running ratio. Your record stays intact.

Toggle label: Show drift indicator.

04 · The privacy model

Reads patterns, never keystrokes.

The product does not phone home. Your record is yours: it sits in this browser, you can export it as a file, and you can clear it whenever you like. There are no accounts. There is no server.
